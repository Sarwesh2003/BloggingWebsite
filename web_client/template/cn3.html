Rise of Automotive Hacking

Modern cars have dozens of computers, not just for navigating and playing music. Computers monitor and control almost every system in the vehicle, including steering, braking, and the engine itself. That's why automotive cybersecurity is essential. If the vehicle's computer system is not properly protected, hackers can steal data or control the vehicle. As you can imagine, this makes automotive cybersecurity a major concern for consumers, automotive companies, and OEMs. As mentioned earlier, modern vehicles rely heavily on computer systems to monitor and control the various systems of the vehicle. If the car's technology is insecure, hackers can steal data. B. Track your location via GPS. But it gets even more scary. Hackers can even execute commands and subdue the vehicle to the hacker instead of the driver.

The idea that your data is stolen is anxious, but the idea that a hacker controls your car is really scary. Hackers can use commands to enable or disable various features such as air conditioning and wipers, and to control steering, braking and the engine. Modern motors these days come full of computerized software program developing seamless connectivity for drivers in cruise manipulate, engine timing, door lock, airbags and superior structures for motive force assistance. These motors use Bluetooth and WiFi technology to speak that still opens them to numerous vulnerabilities or threats from hackers. Gaining manipulate of the car or the use of microphones for eavesdropping is predicted to upward push in 2022 with greater use of computerized motors. Selfdriving or independent motors use a good in addition complicated mechanism that calls for strict cybersecurity measures.
