Rise of Automotive Hacking

Modern cars are equipped with dozens of computers, not just for navigating and playing music. Computers monitor and control almost every system in the vehicle, including steering, brakes, and the engine itself. That's why automotive cybersecurity is essential. If the vehicle's computer system is not properly protected, hackers can steal data or control the vehicle. As you can imagine, this makes automotive cybersecurity a major concern for consumers, automotive companies, and OEMs. As mentioned earlier, modern vehicles rely heavily on computer systems to monitor and control the various systems of the vehicle. If the car's technology is insecure, hackers can steal data. B. Track your location via GPS. But it gets even more scary. Hackers can also execute orders and create vehicles that are targeted by hackers rather than drivers. The idea of ​​data being stolen is scary, but the idea of ​​a hacker controlling your car is really scary. Hackers can use commands to enable or disable various features such as air conditioning and wipers, and to control steering, braking and the engine. Today's engines are full of computer-aided software programs that develop seamless driver connections in the areas of cruising operation, engine control, door locks, airbags, and superior power assist structures. Although these engines communicate using Bluetooth and WiFi technologies, they are still exposed to numerous vulnerabilities and threats from hackers. Manipulating a car or tapping with a microphone is expected to increase the use of computer-controlled engines in 2022 and turn upwards. Self-propelled or independent engines use highly complex mechanisms that require strict cybersecurity measures.
