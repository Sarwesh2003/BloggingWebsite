Advantages Of Big Data

  • Better decision-making: By using data-driven analytics, companies can gain data-driven insights that will help them compete and grow.
  • Increased productivity: Using modern big data tools, analysts can analyze data more quickly and more thoroughly, enhancing their productivity. Moreover, the insights gained from those analyses are often used to improve productivity across the organization.
  • Fraud detection: Financial services companies use big data analytics to detect fraud - an important use for the technology. Systems equipped with machine learning and big data analytics can detect patterns and anomalies exceptionally well, which is one of their biggest advantages.
    The ability to detect stolen credit cards or fraudulent purchases often gives banks and credit card companies an edge before the cardholder is aware that something is amiss.
  • Increased agility: These organizations use big data to better align their IT and business efforts and to impact their business strategies and tactics at a faster and more frequent pace.

Disadvantages Of Big Data

  • Data Quality: IFor data scientists and analysts, accuracy, relevance, and the correct format of the information to analyze matters a lot. As a result, reporting processes are slowed considerably, but enterprises might find their analytics useless if they don't resolve data quality issues.
  • Cybersecurity risks: Companies storing big data, especially sensitive data, may become more attractive targets for cybercriminals.
  • Hardware needs: Big data analytics initiatives can be complicated by implementing an IT infrastructure that is capable of supporting them.The cost of acquiring and maintaining storage space to store the data, network bandwidth to transport it to and from analytics systems, and computing resources to perform those analytics are all high.
